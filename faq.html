Frequently Asked Questions

How does the "Privacy Scrubber" actually work?

The scrubber uses a client-side RegEx (Regular Expression) engine that identifies patterns matching Student IDs, Full Names, and Email addresses. It redacts these *before* they are sent to any AI API, ensuring your identity never leaves your browser.

Does this prevent AI detection by teachers?

Guardian AI isn't built to "hide" AI usage, but to encourage *ethical* usage. By using Vector Logic warnings, students are alerted when their work is too close to a copy-paste AI response, prompting them to rephrase and think critically.

Is my data stored on Guardian AI servers?

No. Our framework follows a "Zero-Retention" policy. All processing happens in the local execution environment (the Sandbox), and no data is logged or stored long-term.

Can I use this for any AI model?

Yes. The Guardian framework is designed as an "interception layer" that can sit in front of ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude, or any LLM, acting as a security buffer.

Why was the 6-3-5 method used?

The 6-3-5 method allowed us to rapidly iterate through 108 ideas in a very short time, ensuring that we didn't just pick the easiest solution, but the most viable and ethical one.
